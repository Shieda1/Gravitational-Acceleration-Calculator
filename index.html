What is the gravitational acceleration?
The gravitational acceleration is a term that indicates the intensity of a gravitational field. It is expressed by the leeter and it's unit is m/s^2.
How to calculate gravitational acceleration?
To calculate gravitational acceleration you should use the following formula:
g = -((GM * r^) / r^2)
where:
g = gravitational acceleration
G = gravitational constant
M = mass of the field sourse
r = distance between the two point-like masses
r^ = unit vector directed from the field source to the smaller sample mass.

The gravitational acceleration calculator is used in order to measure the intensity of a gravitational field.
